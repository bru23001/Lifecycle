# Phase 3 — Project Evaluation and Selection

## 1. Purpose

Rank and **select** which initiatives proceed past early framing (Phases 1–2) using **evidence-backed** opportunity analysis: market context, competition, trends, risks, and strategic fit. Phase 3 produces the **selection signal** and evidence package that feeds **Phase 4 — Feasibility and Business Case**; selection plus feasibility together complete **G3 — Project Selected** in `21. Decision Gates.md` (corresponds to legacy **UG1 — Selection** in `Universal Software Project Development Procedure.md`).

## 2. Scoring Matrix

Use the scoring matrix to compare candidate initiatives consistently before selecting one for feasibility. Adjust weights only when the portfolio governance owner approves the rationale.

| Criterion | Weight | Score 1 | Score 3 | Score 5 | Evidence |
| --- | ---: | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Strategic alignment | 20% | Weak or unclear fit | Supports one stated priority | Directly advances a priority or mandate | Strategy notes, sponsor rationale |
| User / customer value | 20% | Limited or unvalidated value | Clear value for defined users | High-value pain point with strong validation | Problem Definition Document, interviews, tickets |
| Feasibility confidence | 15% | Major unknowns or blockers | Feasible with manageable unknowns | Feasible with strong evidence and known path | Technical notes, SME review |
| Risk and compliance exposure | 15% | High unmanaged risk | Known risks with mitigation path | Low or well-controlled risk | Risk notes, standards applicability |
| Complexity fit | 10% | Complexity exceeds current capacity | Complexity manageable with planning | Complexity fits team/process capacity | Complexity estimate, staffing assumptions |
| Cost / benefit signal | 10% | Weak or unclear return | Plausible return with assumptions | Strong return or necessary investment | Forecasts, cost notes, benefit model |
| Timing / urgency | 10% | Low urgency or poor timing | Useful within normal planning window | Time-sensitive or opportunity-critical | Market signal, operational deadline |

**Scoring guidance:** Multiply each criterion score by its weight, rank candidates by total weighted score, then record the recommendation: **Proceed to Feasibility**, **Pivot**, **Defer**, or **Stop**. A high score does not override unresolved evidence gaps; mark those as assumptions or blockers for Phase 4.

---

## 3. Entry criteria

- **G2 — Problem Validated** outcome is **Validated — Proceed to Selection**.
- A completed **Problem Definition Document** (Template A-0.1) and upstream **Idea Capture Form** are available.
- Sponsorship or a nominated owner can commit effort to research and scoring.

## 4. Required inputs

- **Idea Capture Form** (Template A-0).
- **Problem Definition Document** (Template A-0.1), including validated problem statement, stakeholders, and pain-point summary.
- Current complexity estimate and rationale from Phases 1–2 (`23. Project Complexity Levels.md`).
- Initial hypothesis of **in-scope / out-of-scope** (may be revised later).
- Strategic priorities or portfolio constraints (if any).

## 5. Activities

### 5.1 Market and opportunity research

Produce a **Business Field Report** (or an approved equivalent) using **Template A-4** in `28. Appendix A — Template Library.md`. Cover market size and segmentation, customer behavior, competitive landscape, financial signals where relevant, SWOT, trends, challenges, growth areas, and **actionable recommendations**. Tailor depth to portfolio rules (small initiatives may use a lighter variant if formally waived).

### 5.2 Evaluation and comparison

- Define **selection criteria** (e.g. strategic alignment, customer value, technical feasibility sketch, risk, rough cost/benefit).
- Score or rank **project candidates** using **Template A-3.1 — Project Selection Scorecard** or a program-standard equivalent.
- Use complexity as an evaluation input for expected documentation depth, review effort, delivery risk, staffing, cost/benefit, and feasibility burden.
- Identify whether each candidate is tied to an existing product (`PRD-XXX`), is likely to become a new product requiring PRCS registration, or is pre-product/cross-cutting work that should use Work Type Tag(s) until a product record exists.
- Compare candidates when multiple options exist; record **why** the winner was chosen.

### 5.3 Decision packaging

Summarize the Phase 3 recommendation: **Proceed to Feasibility**, **Pivot**, **Defer**, or **Stop**. Link evidence (field report, scorecard, assumptions) to the recommendation for auditability. The full **G3** decision (**Proceed to Requirements**, **Pivot**, **Defer**, or **Stop**) is recorded after Phase 4 completes feasibility and business-case evidence.

## 6. Required outputs

- **Business Field Report** (Template A-4) **or** documented **waiver** with rationale (only when program policy allows).
- **Project Selection Scorecard** (Template A-3.1) or equivalent **project evaluation record**.
- Minimum evaluation fields: selection criteria, scoring method, candidate list, score/rank, current complexity level, PRCS context (`PRD-XXX` if existing, candidate product flag, likely domain tags, Work Type Tag rationale if pre-product/cross-cutting), recommendation, rationale, dependencies/assumptions, waiver if any, reviewer, and date.
- Inputs ready for Phase 4 (feasibility study and business case).

## 7. Decision gates

- **Proceed to Feasibility (Phase 4):** Phase 3 selection recommendation approved; evidence sufficient to begin the funding and feasibility conversation.
- **Defer / stop:** documented rationale; triggers for revisit if applicable.

Phase 3 does **not** complete G3 by itself. Phase 3 supplies the selection evidence; **Phase 4** supplies feasibility and business-case evidence. Together they support the **G3 — Project Selected** decision in `21. Decision Gates.md`.

## 8. Roles responsible

- Product or business owner: prioritization and sponsor alignment.
- Strategy or portfolio governance: criteria and gate consistency (when assigned).
- Engineering or architecture: lightweight technical realism check for scoring.
- SMEs or analysts: market/competitive inputs as needed.

## 9. Quality checks

- Claims in the field report are **traceable** to sources (primary/secondary).
- SWOT and recommendations are **actionable**, not generic.
- Selection criteria applied **consistently** across competing candidates.
- Scoring matrix weights, scores, and evidence are documented or explicitly replaced by an approved equivalent selection method.
- Complexity is included in evaluation scoring, risk, expected evidence depth, or documented as not relevant with rationale.
- Project Selection Scorecard or equivalent record includes criteria, scoring method, candidate list, recommendation, rationale, dependencies/assumptions, reviewer, and date.
- No full **G3** approval without both Phase 3 selection evidence and Phase 4 feasibility/business-case evidence.

## 10. Exit criteria

- Decision recorded; artifacts stored under change control.
- Phase 4 can begin with a clear **candidate** and supporting evidence.
