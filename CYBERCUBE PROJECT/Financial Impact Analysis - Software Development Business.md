## 2. Net Present Value (NPV) Analysis

### 2.1 Methodology

**Discount Rate:** 12% (reflects early-stage business risk, opportunity cost of founder salary forgone, and market risk premium for services businesses)

**Cash Flow Components:**
- **Initial Investment:** Upfront capital required (Months 0–3)
- **Operating Cash Flow:** EBITDA + non-cash adjustments (depreciation minimal for services)
- **Working Capital Changes:** Accounts receivable growth (30–60 day payment terms typical)
- **Terminal Value:** Exit valuation at Year 5 (services 1.5–2× revenue, SaaS/platform 8–12× ARR)

### 7.2 Capital Deployment Strategy

**Phase 1 (Months 0–12): Minimize Burn, Maximize Learning**

- Start with **Scenario 2 budget** ($85K initial capital) to extend runway
- Founder living expenses: $4–6K/month (bare minimum, supplement with consulting if needed)
- Delay all optional spending: No office, no sales rep, minimal marketing (bootstrap SEO + outbound)
- Prioritize: AI tools ($1.5–2K/mo), insurance ($500/mo), CRM/infra ($300/mo)
- **Goal:** Close 3–6 projects at $50–100K each, validate product-market fit, build RSM library v1

**Phase 2 (Months 12–24): Scale to Profitability**

- Reinvest 100% of gross profit into growth (defer founder salary increase until Month 18)
- Hire fractional QA ($10–15K/mo) at Month 12 to double validation capacity
- Increase marketing spend to $5–10K/mo (content, ads, partnerships)
- Hire sales rep at Month 18–20 ($150K OTE, 10–15% commission on gross profit)
- **Goal:** 10–15 projects/year, breakeven or profitable by Month 18, RSM reuse 50–60%

**Phase 3 (Months 24–36): Productization + Expansion**

- Module marketplace launch (Month 24–30): Invest $50–75K in catalog, licensing, docs
- SaaS pilot (Month 30–36): $100–150K investment in multi-tenant architecture, 10–15 beta customers
- Founder salary to market rate ($180–220K/yr) once profitable
- Expand QA team to 3–4 fractional specialists ($40–60K/mo blended)
- **Goal:** $2.5–4M revenue, 20–25 projects/year, $300–500K module + SaaS ARR

### 7.3 Fundraising vs. Bootstrapping Decision

**Bootstrap if:**
- Founder has sufficient personal capital ($85K–$210K) or low burn tolerance ($3–5K/mo living expenses)
- Want to retain 100% equity and control
- Can tolerate slower growth (4–6 projects Year 1 vs. 10–15 with funding)
- Willing to do all sales + delivery + operations initially

**Raise Pre-Seed ($250K–$500K) if:**
- Want faster growth (hire sales + QA immediately, aggressive marketing)
- Need personal income runway (pay founder $100–150K salary from Day 1)
- Want validator/advisor network (investors bring intros, credibility)
- Comfortable with 10–20% dilution (post-money valuation $2.5–5M)

**Raise Seed ($1–2M) at Month 18–24 if:**
- Strong traction (10–15 projects closed, $1–2M revenue run rate, 55–60% gross margin)
- Want to accelerate SaaS productization (hire full-time product + engineering team)
- Plan to move upmarket (enterprise sales team, compliance investments, SOC 2)
- Targeting strategic exit in 3–5 years (prefer faster growth over bootstrapped profitability)

**Recommendation for Risk-Averse Founder:**  
Bootstrap through profitability (Month 18), then optionally raise growth capital to accelerate SaaS transition. Avoids early-stage valuation risk (pre-revenue raise at $2–3M valuation vs. post-traction raise at $10–15M valuation) and preserves founder equity (80–90% vs. 50–60% post-Series A).

## Appendix A: Financial Model Assumptions Detail

### Revenue Assumptions

**Project Volume Growth Drivers:**
- Year 1: Founder-led sales only, 40–60% discovery → proposal conversion, 30–50% proposal → close
- Year 2: +Sales rep (Month 18–20), referrals accelerate (20% of pipeline), case studies reduce sales cycle 30%
- Year 3+: Inbound marketing matures (30% of pipeline), enterprise customers (2–3× startup ACV), retainers stabilize revenue

**Average Deal Size Progression:**
- Startup tier: $35–75K (MVP, integrations, <8 weeks)
- SMB tier: $75–140K (moderate web apps, cloud migration, 8–12 weeks)
- Mid-market tier: $140–250K (enterprise features, compliance, custom architecture, 12–16 weeks)
- Enterprise tier: $250–500K+ (mission-critical, regulated, multi-quarter, post-Year 3)

**Mix Shift (% of Projects by Tier):**
- Year 1: 60% Startup, 35% SMB, 5% Mid-Market, 0% Enterprise
- Year 2: 40% Startup, 40% SMB, 18% Mid-Market, 2% Enterprise
- Year 3: 25% Startup, 35% SMB, 30% Mid-Market, 10% Enterprise
- Year 5: 15% Startup, 25% SMB, 40% Mid-Market, 20% Enterprise

### Cost Assumptions

**AI Compute (per project):**
- MVP-tier (4–6 weeks): $2–5K tokens (GPT-4 for architecture, GPT-3.5 for routine, Codex for test gen)
- Standard (8–10 weeks): $5–10K tokens
- Complex (12–16 weeks): $10–20K tokens
- Blended average: 3–5% of project revenue

**Human QA Hours (per project):**
- Year 1 (no RSM, manual): 150–250 hrs @ $150–200/hr loaded = $22.5–50K per $100K project (22–50% COGS)
- Year 2 (RSM 40–50% reuse): 100–150 hrs = $15–30K per $100K project (15–30% COGS)
- Year 3+ (RSM 60–70% reuse): 50–100 hrs = $7.5–20K per $100K project (8–20% COGS)

**SG&A Allocation:**
- Founder compensation: 40–50% of SG&A
- Sales/marketing: 25–35% of SG&A
- Admin/office/legal/other: 15–25% of SG&A

### Exit Valuation Assumptions

**Services Multiple Drivers:**
- Base case: 1.5× revenue (typical for sub-$5M agencies)
- Premium (+0.3–0.5×): Recurring revenue >30%, gross margin >60%, enterprise customers >40%, compliance-ready (SOC 2)
- Discount (-0.2–0.3×): Customer concentration >30%, founder dependency high, no management team

**SaaS Multiple Drivers:**
- Base case: 8× ARR (for $1–5M ARR SaaS, 85%+ gross margin, <5% churn, 50–100% YoY growth)
- Premium (10–12× ARR): Strategic buyer, >100% YoY growth, <3% churn, enterprise customers, network effects
- Discount (5–7× ARR): High churn (>7%), slowing growth (<30% YoY), competitive market, low NPS
